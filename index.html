Sober Rob

Sober rob began playing the viola when he was eight years old. The sheet music and chin-rests have been traded in for Ableton and KRK’s, but the compositional elements of his classical background still course through the hyper-futuristic style of his production. At points, sober rob's music sounds like a corroding machine trying to speak like a human, combining sporadic and distorted squeaks with synth glitches and rattling basses while incorporating engaging melodic harmonies. Listening to it feels like you’re doing something you shouldn’t be allowed to. His untraditional composition and incomparable sound design is already not-so-quietly disrupting a scene that imbibes 'what’s hot' by allowing his audience to preview what’s next before it arrives. ​ Ultimately, sober rob is striving to set an example for how limitless the possibilities of musical expression actually are.

Matt Kali

Matt Kali’s music blends futuristic production elements with dark pop melodies and rhythmic, laid back vocals. His remix of 'Starboy' is the third most popular in the world, and he's racked up over 10 million hits across platforms. His records have been featured in commercials for Samsung, The Voice, Saturday Night Live, Disney, NBC News, The Today Show, Nestea, and LL Bean among many others. Matt Kali is the current representative of the disillusioned youth - guarded yet vulnerable, endearing but mysterious, nostalgic and somehow completely brand new, all at the exact same time.

Ben Zaidi

Ben Zaidi's music combines the lyrical solemnity of spoken-word poetry with minimal electronica. Written and produced by Ben in his Seattle bedroom, his songs reveal the sincere intimacy of a private journal. Zaidi grew up surrounded by the natural serenity of the Pacific Northwest, before traveling east to study poetry at Harvard. He recently broke the airwaves on Seattle's iconic radio station KEXP 90.3 and completed his debut tour. Ben now resides primarily in Brooklyn, New York. His music is a story of endings - some as carefree as summer, complicated as love, and final as life. A solitary hopeful stream running through a damaged land, reminding us that within loss there lies the possibility of self-discovery.
